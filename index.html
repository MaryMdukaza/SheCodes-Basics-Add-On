Digital art refers to any artistic work or practice that uses digital technology as part of the creative or presentation process. It can also refer to computational art that uses and engages with digital media. Since the 1960s, various names have been used to describe digital art, including computer art, electronic art, multimedia art, and new media art.

I love digital art because it gives me endless creative freedom. With just a tablet and software, I can experiment with colors, textures, and effects that would be difficult in traditional mediums. The ability to undo mistakes and work in layers makes the process more flexible and enjoyable. Plus, digital art allows me to create anywhere, share my work instantly, and explore different styles without limitations. It's a perfect blend of technology and creativity, making every piece a new adventure. üòä
